The 2025/2026 rainfall season is likely to be partly influenced by **Neutral El Niño–Southern Oscillation (ENSO)** conditions in the tropical Pacific Ocean, among other climatic factors. Overall, **Zomba District is expected to receive generally normal to above-normal cumulative rainfall** during the 2025/2026 rainfall season.

Rainfall onset for the season is expected from the **last week of November**, while cessation is likely to occur in **early April**. The total season length is projected to last **between 120 and 130 days**. There is a **low likelihood of dry spells lasting more than one week in January**, but a **higher likelihood of dry spells exceeding one week in February**.

In **October 2025**, most areas are expected to receive total rainfall amounts of **around 50 mm**, which falls within the **normal to above-normal range** for this month. These early rains, locally known as *Chizimalupsya*, are expected to be accompanied by **strong, gusty winds and lightning**.

During **November 2025**, rainfall amounts between **50 and 100 mm** are anticipated across the district. The rains are also expected to be accompanied by **strong winds and lightning**, with the main seasonal rainfall onset expected in the **last week of November**.

In **December 2025**, most areas are expected to receive rainfall amounts of up to **250 mm**, which is within the **normal to above-normal range**. **Zomba City** is projected to receive approximately **300 mm** of rainfall, while most areas in **Traditional Authority (TA) Mlumbe** are expected to receive around **200 mm**.

For **January 2026**, Zomba District is expected to receive **high rainfall amounts ranging between 300 and 350 mm**. The **highest rainfall totals are anticipated in TA Kuntumanji and TA Mlumbe**. However, some areas in **TA Mwambo** may experience **short dry spells lasting no more than one week**.

In **February 2026**, most areas are expected to receive **lower rainfall amounts of about 200 mm**, which is considered **normal to below-normal** for this month. There is a likelihood of **dry spells lasting up to two weeks**, particularly over **TA Mwambo and areas around Chikamveka**.

During **March 2026**, rainfall amounts of **around 200 mm** are expected across most parts of the district. These amounts are **higher than the long-term average** for this month, indicating a relatively wet period before the end of the season.

By **April 2026**, most areas are expected to receive **less than 100 mm of rainfall**, marking the end of the rainy season. **Rainfall cessation is expected to occur from around early April**.
